Spectrum Browser

Previous | Next | See Also

Spectrum Browser allows you gain an insight on the spectrum you interested, the main display area shows the peak list which you can interact with. On right hand side of the browser, there is also a toolbar which offers a number of convenient commands.

Spectrum Browser's features can be classified into the following four categories:

  • Basic interactions
  • Display Mass Differences
  • Display Ion Annotations
  • Show Spectrum MetaData

Basic interactions

Using Spectrum Browser, you can Save, Print, Export, Zoom, Show Grid, Highlight the spectrum of your interest.

Save: On the tool bar, click on , you can save the spectrum to your disk as a PNG image.

Print: On the tool bar, click on , you can print the spectrum as a PNG image.

Export: On the tool bar, click on , you can export the peak list m/z and intensity values into a tab delimited file.

Zoom: To zoom in, you can left click and hold your mouse key, drag to highlight the area you want to zoom into. To zoom out, you can either right click your mouse in the main display area or on the tool bar, click on .

Show Grid: On the tool bar, click on , this will show/hide the grid in the main display area.

Highlight: You can highlight the peak you are interested by mouse over it, on the top left corner of the main display area, the m/z and intensity value will be displayed. The peak will be highlighted with m/z value. The tooltip will also show all the related details about that peak.

Display Mass Differences

To trigger a start of the highlighting mass difference, you can left click your mouse on a peak of your interest. Move the mouse to other peaks, a range will be drawn with the mass difference. If one or many amino acids match the mass difference, they will displayed with the correct charge as well.

To clear all existing mass differences, click on .

Display Ion Annotations

If fragment ions information is provided for the spectrum you selected, they will be highlighted in the main display area. Spectrum Browser will also annotate the possible matching amino acids within the ion series.

You can filter the ion series or amino acid annotations by clicking .

Click will show all the metadata related to the selected spectrum.
